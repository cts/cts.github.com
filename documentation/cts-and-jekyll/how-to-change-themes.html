Theminator

Right now, you only have one theme installed, Mog. We're going to show you how to browse and preview themes; you can see how themes look on your blog, without even have to install them in your directory.

First, drag this button up to your favorites toolbar:

Navigate to your blog. This could be the up-and-running site on the web, or it could be running locally.

How? If you want to run your site from a local server, you can run jekyll serve in your blog's root directory, which will build the site in the _site folder, then put it on the development server. The default port is 4000 (so you'd go to localhost:4000), but you can specify something different in _config.yml.
Find out more about building and serving your site here, or about deploying your site here.

Click on your bookmarklet to open the theminator sidebar.

The Theminator will let you browse through all of the themes that are currently compatible with CTS.




If you already know the name of the theme you want, you can search for it.



Wordpress themes are described by their colors, columns, width, features, and subject. To search for a theme with specfic tags, check the boxes and press Filter.




Hover your mouse over a screenshot to see more information about a theme. You'll be able to see the name, Preview and Install buttons, and a Favorites icon (the star). To add a theme to your favorites, just click the star. To remove it, click the star again. If you want to display all of your favorite themes, press the star next to the search field.


To preview a theme, simply click Preview and watch as CTS transforms your blog. To revert it back to how it was, just deselect the Preview button. Once you decide on a theme, click Install, and we'll add all the files you need and change the theme property in _config.yml for you.


Terminal

You can also add new themes through your terminal.

Navigate to your blog's root directory and run:

cts add-theme blog theme

where theme is whatever theme you chose. We'll set up the files and change the theme property in _config.yml --just like if you were to press the Install button in the Theminator.



Now you're all ready to use CTS with Jekyll!
